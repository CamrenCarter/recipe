Fried Chicken

Ingredients Directions
2 whole free-range, organic chickens Cut the whole chickens into 4 breasts, 4 thighs, 4 legs and 4 wings and set aside.
Vegetable oil of choice, for frying the chicken Preheat your oil, in either a heavy pan on the stove or a deep-fryer, to 325 degrees F.
6 cups all-purpose flour In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder and cayenne pepper until thoroughly mixed. Set aside.
5 tablespoons salt Pour the buttermilk into another bowl large enough for the chicken to be immersed in the buttermilk.
4 tablespoons ground black pepper Prepare your dredging station. Place your chicken in a bowl. Next to that, your bowl of buttermilk, and next to that, your dry mixture.
2 tablespoons garlic powder Take your breasts, lightly dust them with your flour mixture, then dip them in the buttermilk until they are coated, and then place them in the flour mixture.
1 tablespoon onion powder Take the breasts that are in the flour mixture and aggressively push the flour mixture into the wet chicken. Make sure that the chicken in very thoroughly coated, or you will not achieve the crust and crunch you are looking for. Gently place the breasts in your hot oil.
2 teaspoons cayenne pepper Next, repeat the dredging steps with your other pieces of chicken in this order: thigh, leg then wing.
2 cups buttermilk When you place the last wing into the fryer, you should have 16 pieces of chicken in the oil. Set a timer for 15 minutes.
After 15 minutes, take a probe thermometer and check the temperature of a breast. If it reads 180 degrees F, all of your chicken is done. (Keep in mind that it will continue to cook after it has been removed from the fryer.)
Remove the chicken from the oil and let it drain for 5 minutes. Let cool for an additional 10 minutes before serving.

Sweet Patatoe Pie

Ingredients Directions
Filling Sprinkle in 1 1/4 cup of all purpose flour into a large mixing bowl.
1 lb sweet potatoes peeled & chopped Add in the cold butter, butter flavored shortening, 2 tbsp of sugar, 1 tsp salt, and 1 tsp of vanilla extract.
2 medium sized eggs Mix the ingredients until it forms into a dough, then wrap with plastic wrap and store the dough in the refrigerator.
1 tbsp pure vanilla extract Toss the peeled and chopped sweet potatoes into a medium sized pot, and pour in about 4-6 cups of water.
1 tsp ground cinnamon Place the pot over high heat, and boil the potatoes, until they are fork tender.
1/2 tsp salt Once the potatoes are done, drain the water, and let the potatoes cool.
